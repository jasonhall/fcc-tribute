Miyamoto Musashi History's Greatest Swordsman

Miyamoto Musashi could have been the greatest swordsman who ever lived, but much information about him is not certain. He came from a time of limited records, and his own writings and art were only about strategy, swordsmanship, and spirituality.

About Miyamoto Musashi

  1. He was born around 1584 in a village called Miyamoto in the province of Harima. He took his name from the village and his original name is not clear.
  2. His father was a samurai, but the identity of his mother is not certain.
  3. He likely learned swordsmanship originally from his father. He learned to read and write, and abut Zen Buddhism from an uncle.
  4. He won his first duel against a samurai at age 13, and his second at around 16.
  5. He was on the losing side of a battle and survived, but became ronin - a samurai without a master.
  6. He traveled around Japan for many years perfecting his skills and participating in duels against accomplished swordsmen. (Most duels were to determine the better martial artist - not to kill the opponent.)
  7. He usually arrived late - perhaps in order to anger his opponent.
  8. He developed a system of using two swords rather than one.
  9. In later years, he usually used a wooden sword, regardless of what his opponent used - even if the duel was to be to death.
  10. He is credited with over 60 known duels, with others uncounted, such as on battlefields - winning every one.
  11. He was an accomplished artist, poet, and strategist.
  12. While living as a hermit, he wrote "The Book of Five Rings" - a famous book about strategy that is still studied today and applied to other fields, such as business.
  13. He is believed to have died from lung cancer past the age of 60.
